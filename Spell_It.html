SPELL IT

We created a mobile app where kids learn to spell using speech recognition in a playful, engaging way.

UI/UX Designer

Timeframe: 3 weeks

Our Process

Getting to know the goals, needs and users of Narika.org.

The Power of Voice

Studies show that oral language development is an effective way of learning how to spell.


This type of learning is particularly beneficial for students with dyslexia and dysgraphia.


Insights

To discover the goals and pain points of our target users, we interviewed 4 parents with kids aged 9-12 and 3 children aged 9.

“I find it challenging to teach my kids spelling.”

- Zhenya (mom of three)

“Kids get irritated if their parents force them to practice spelling.”

- Priya (mom of two)

“Getting rewards will motivate me to learn spelling.”

- Sanchi (9 years old)

User Flow

Low Fidelity Homepage

For usability testing of the new design for the website, We recruited users and asked them to perform some tasks & “think aloud” during the process using the prototype.

  • Implement chatbot
  • Improve banner image and content
  • Highlight safety exit
  • Reduce the size of dropdown width
  • Move news in the top nav
  • Add dates on the event
  • Add program details on the cards

Final Thoughts and Future Features

Testing participants provided essential insights and suggestions. We would like to continue to provide voice assistance to teach kids to spell in an engaging way.

In the future we would consider making additional changes such as:

  • Connect with friends
  • Add more lessons and games
  • Parent Profiles
  • Invite friends to play games with you
  • Add customized spelling lists
  • Add times intervals to monitor time spent
