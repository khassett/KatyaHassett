SPELL IT

We created a mobile app where kids learn to spell using speech recognition in a playful, engaging way.

UI/UX Designer

Timeframe: 3 weeks

Team:

Katya Hassett, Kavya Signh, Renu Vaswani, Harry Morgan

Our Process

Project Overview

Studies show that oral language development is an effective way of learning how to spell.

This type of learning is particularly beneficial for students with dyslexia and dyraphia.


Problem Statement:

Many children find learning to spell boring and repetitive, and struggle to retain words through reading and writing alone.

Insights

To discover the goals and pain points of our target users, we interviewed
4 parents with kids aged 9-12 and 3 children aged 9.

“I find it challenging to teach my kids spelling.”

- Zhenya (mom of three)

“Kids get irritated if their parents force them to practice spelling.”

- Priya (mom of two)

“Getting rewards will motivate me to learn spelling.”

- Sanchi (9 years old)

Survey Results

We distributed a survey using Google Forms and received
8 responses

62%say their child has trouble spelling.

80%say their child currently uses an app for spelling.

72%use phonics or a combination of oral + verbal methods

Final Thoughts and Future Features

Testing participants provided essential insights and suggestions. We would like to continue to provide voice assistance to teach kids to spell in an engaging way.

In the future we would consider making additional changes such as:

  • Connect with friends

  • Add more lessons and games

  • Parent Profiles

  • Invite friends to play games with you

  • Add customized spelling lists

  • Add times intervals to monitor time spent
