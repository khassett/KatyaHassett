Smart Course

Smart Course offers engaging online courses, masterclasses, and a collaborative platform for caregivers of kids with special needs.

My Role: UI/UX Developer

Skills: Research, User Flows, Information Architecture, Sketching, Wireframing, Branding UI, Prototyping

Timeline: 2 months

Problem

Former sales page had a high bounce rate and usability issues. We used HotJar and Google Analytics to see how users were interacting with Smart Course products.

Challenges

As a startup, the client had a slim budget for design. As this was a constraint I could not control, I made the most of the time allotted. As the business grows, this would be the first step in many. To that end, there wasn’t a lot of user flows to work with to get started. The founder wanted me to redesign pages that were already built, so I made sure to understand what users want to achieve when going through the site.

Goals

Increase user retention. Improve KPIs each week. Increase MRR by 10x.

Users

Caregivers for students with ADHD

Experts living to deliver masterclasses

Target User Persona

48 years old

Seattle, WA

Teacher

Motivations

Find easy and accessible ADHD resources to help her sons succeed. Sign up for courses to learn from ADHD experts. Support her sons academically and at home.

Goals

Find easy and accessible ADHD resources to help her sons succeed. Sign up for courses to learn from ADHD experts. Support her sons academically and at home.

Pain Points

Doesn’t have time to do a lot of research in ADHD care. Is not very tech savvy and gets frusturated when websites are hard to navigate. Doesn’t have a lot of time in her schedule.

The original Homepage

After testing and using HotJar Analytics we narrowed down the most important fixes to reduce the lightbox bounce rate.

1. Poor contrast

2. Not making the most of the page to help users understand the mission of Smart Course.

3. Low contrast, lines aren't aligned, poor visual design.

Redesigning the HomePage

I looked through competitors and worked with my project manager to find features that we felt were important to include on the Smart Course homepage.

1. Include the work ADHD above the fold because that is what Smart Course is currently focused on.

2. Clear call to actions and clarifying Smart Course goals for users.

3. Clean graphics, high contrast, and an in depth explanation of how Smart Course products work.

It was important for the co-founder of SmartCourse to include our sponsors above the fold because it adds credibility and shows who we work with.

Due to time constraints with the Smart Course ADHD summit approaching, I was asked to bring these features into high-fidelity as soon as possible. I like to do user testing in grayscale and low-fidelity designs to continue iterating throughout the process. In this case, I started adding graphics and images to Figma to present to the produt team.

Redesigning the Sales Page Lightbox

The current pop-up had a high bounce rate and was hard for users to understand. I worked on redesigning this lightbox on Figma and then added it onto Wix. I worked with the product team to redesign this lightbox.

After testing and using HotJar Analytics we narrowed down the most important fixes to reduce the lightbox bounce rate.

1. Difficult to navigate between monthly and yearly payments.

2. Unclear price points, not highlighting the most appealing plan for users.

3. Low contrast, lines aren't aligned, poor visual design.

After solidiying these pain points, I made the following changes:

1. As users read from left to right, the lightbox highlights the yearly plan (less expensive in the long run) on the left side.

2. I made sure the elements were aligned and centered.

3. I worked with the co-founder to list the features from most to least useful for our target users.

Coding dropdown features

I worked with software engineer Ally Pettit to design and develop the dropdowns on Wix using javascript.

Final Thoughts and Future Features

Our testing participants and stakeholders provided a lot of helpful insights and suggestions. Given the time frame we had, we were unable to implement all of these changes. In the future, we would like to continue building an emotional connection with users, focusing on providing a holistic understanding of domestic violence and intuitive ways for users to seek help or get involved.

In the future we would consider making additional changes such as:

  • - Building out the rest of the pages

  • - Event Registration (more intuitive event sign up process)

  • - Member onboarding

  • - Employment opportunities
