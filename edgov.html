Navigation Usability Plan

Objectives: How do users navigate through the website?
Do the navigation categories conform with users’ expectations?

    Questions

  • Without looking at the homepage, what do you expect to find on the site?

  • What kind of information do you think you’ll find on each page? (Ask for each tab)

  • Imagine you’re a current grad student seeking to repay your loans from college. How would you go about accomplishing this?

  • If you are also looking to take out additional loans and grants to pay for grad school, what would you do on this site to accomplish that?

User Insights

“I don’t like having to click and go to a new page, I would rather have access to everything on one page.”
“’Data’ is the least clear, but I have a decent idea of what the headers mean.”
“I’m a 31 year old educated woman, imagine what a 16-17 year old feels like, or a parent who isn’t college educated.”
“The most relevant to me would be: ‘Do you want money? Fill out the FAFSA.’”

Final Thoughts and Future Features

Testing participants provided essential insights and suggestions. We would like to continue to provide voice assistance to teach kids to spell in an engaging way.

In the future we would consider making additional changes such as:

  • Connect with friends
  • Add more lessons and games
  • Parent Profiles
  • Invite friends to play games with you
  • Add customized spelling lists
  • Add times intervals to monitor time spent
