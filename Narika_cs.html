NARIKA

Everyone deserves healthy relationships.

Narika is a non-profit organization founded in 1992 to promote women's empowerment by assisting victims of domestic violence, and trafficking. Narika provides advocacy and support to the women while helping them attain mentally and financial literacy, so they can become self-sufficient.

UI/UX Designer

NonProfit Web Redesign

Stakeholder Interviews

Getting to know the goals, needs and users of Narika.org.

Goals

  • Convey support of women, not anti-men or anti-family
  • Inspire donations and contributions from site visitors
  • Highlight the impact of the work Narika does

Needs

  • Helpline info, contact button and donate button all easily accessible
  • Reorganize information and content
  • Intuitive, easy, not text heavy

Users

  • Domestic Violence survivors
  • Youth eager to contribute and learn
  • Donors, prospective volunteers
  • Partner nonprofits and organizations

Heuristic Evaluation

  • Two call to actions, some navigation points overlap. Narrow down the navigation bar, add items into dropdown menu.
  • Hero image is not clear, should give the user a strong, clear understanding of the webpage.
  • Allies page is distracting from the actual mission of the webpage. Although important for the company to represent their allies, should be in another place.
  • Video is stagnant, user would not play because of the effort. Should play throughout the page on silent to get the message across.

User Interview Plan

Our user research interview plan focused on:


1. How do people feel about the current Narika Website?
2. How accessible, usable, and intuitive is the site?
3. Can users easily volunteer, seek help, and use the navigation bar?
We received the following feedback:
  • No clarity on what the site is about or call to action.
  • Both the navigation bar and footer are long and lengthy.
  • Issues with organization and easy access to content.
  • Hero images are unclear and create confusion throughout the site.
  • “Allies” section placement and carousel speed are awkward.

Low Fidelity Homepage

For usability testing of the new design for the website, We recruited users and asked them to perform some tasks & “think aloud” during the process using the prototype.

  • Implement chatbot
  • Improve banner image and content
  • Highlight safety exit
  • Reduce the size of dropdown width
  • Move news in the top nav
  • Add dates on the event
  • Add program details on the cards

Final Thoughts and Future Features

Our testing participants and stakeholders provided a lot of helpful insights and suggestions. Given the time frame we had, we were unable to implement all of these changes. In the future, we would like to continue building an emotional connection with users, focusing on providing a holistic understanding of domestic violence and intuitive ways for users to seek help or get involved.

In the future we would consider making additional changes such as:

  • Building out the rest of the pages
  • Event Registration (more intuitive event sign up process)
  • Member onboarding
  • Employment opportunities
